    Does this get used against me?

    Without your consent most major web platforms leak whether you are logged in. This allows any website to detect on which platforms you're signed up. Since there are lots of platforms with specific demographics an attacker could reason about your personality, too. This attack could be a step of a worse attack such as deanonymization techniques, Clickjacking, Profilejacking, or Phishing where it is crucial to know if a victim is logged in to a service.

    Prevention: To help keep your browser from leaking your information pertaining to social media and logins. You can enable a private browsing tool depenidng on your browser you are using. Here is a link to a guide that can help get this setup for you. Private Browsing Guide

    What is this data used for?

    Your IP (Internet Protocol) address is your computers personal unique identifiable number. It can be used to gain your location and device. A lot of people worry that their IP address might reveal their name, home address, age, what they look at online and more. That's just not the case. Sure, they might find out some interesting information, but nothing revealing.

    Prevention: There are several online tools used to help hide your IP address such as VPN (Virtual Private Network) sevices. Some examples are ExpressVPN, SurfShark, IPVanish. These can help keep your IP address hidden by encrypting your internet traffic and passing it through a private tunnel to their own secure servers. Allowing no one being able to see what you are reading or downloading.
